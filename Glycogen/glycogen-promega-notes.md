We used the Qiagen Tissue Lyser II with 2-3 mm ceramic beads on whole spat tissue with the PBS/HCl lysis buffer for 50 seconds at 30 shakes/second. Notes indicate that we had more variable homogenization with larger tissues, and we discussed doing longer homogenization in the future, e.g. another 50 seconds. Not sure what homogenizer approach you will take, but uniform homogenization of tissue is important based on discussions with Promega tech support. And again, we froze homogenates at least overnight before proceeding with the assay (defrosted and vortexed before starting assay).
 
Here are the notes detailing procedure from Megan:
 
- Made solution of 5 mL PBS, 2.5 mL HCl (.3N).
- 750 uL of PBS/HCl solution added to lysis bead tubes.
- Dissected oysters and weighed body; shook a few times in tube to break up tissue.
- Placed in tissue lyser for 50 seconds at 30 shakes/second (freq).
- Removed from lyser, added 250 uL TRIS Buffer to each tube (briefly spun down to reduce foam height).
- Placed in -20°C.
